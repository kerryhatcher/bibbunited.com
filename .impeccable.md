## Design Context

### Users
Community residents in BIBB County who want clear, actionable information about their local school system. They arrive on mobile or desktop, often from social media shares, looking to understand what's happening (budgets, policies, board decisions) and know exactly what to do about it (attend meetings, contact officials, get involved). Many are on spotty mobile connections. The content team is 2-3 non-technical editors using the Payload CMS admin panel.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Urgent, Civic-minded.**
- Voice: Direct, no-nonsense editorial tone. Speaks to residents as equals, not down to them.
- Tone: Urgent but not alarmist. Empowering, not preachy.
- Emotional goals: Urgency to act, confidence that action matters, clarity about what to do next.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Newspaper editorial meets civic activism. Sharp, high-contrast, typographically driven. No soft gradients, no rounded corners, no friendly pastel vibes.
- **Typography**: Barlow Condensed (headings, bold, uppercase, tight tracking) + Source Serif 4 (body, serif). The contrast between condensed sans and editorial serif is core to the identity.
- **Color palette**: Navy (#1B2A4A), Crimson (#DC2626), White, warm grays. Two-mode system: "Community Mode" (light, white-dominant) and "Urgent Mode" (dark, navy-dominant).
- **Edges**: Sharp. No border-radius anywhere. Cards use left-accent borders for hierarchy.
- **Imagery**: Full-bleed hero images with dark gradient overlays and bold headline typography.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, pastel wellness sites, purple-blue AI gradients, overly rounded "friendly" UI, Material Design defaults.
- **References**: The Guardian, ProPublica, The Marshall Project -- editorial sites that treat civic information with visual weight and urgency.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** -- Every element must help the reader understand what's happening and what to do. Remove anything that doesn't serve comprehension or action.
2. **Typography is the design** -- The contrast between Barlow Condensed and Source Serif carries the editorial identity. Headlines do the heavy lifting; imagery supports, not leads.
3. **Urgency without alarm** -- Use strong visual weight (bold type, high contrast, crimson accents) to convey importance. But don't cry wolf -- reserve the most intense treatments for genuinely urgent content (UrgentBannerBar).
4. **Sharp edges, sharp message** -- No rounded corners. The visual language should feel like a broadsheet, not a mobile app. Borders and rules create structure the way column lines do in print.
5. **Accessible by default** -- This is a public-interest site. WCAG AA minimum. Focus rings, reduced motion, print styles, semantic HTML -- accessibility is non-negotiable, not a nice-to-have.
